pivotal events

The Discovery of the Death Note

This is the spark that starts the entire series. Light Yagami, a bored but brilliant high school student, finds a black notebook dropped by the Shinigami Ryuk. After testing its power on two criminals, Light realizes the notebook is real. He decides to use it to "cleanse" the world of evil and become the "God of the New World," adopting the alias Kira.

The Lind L. Tailor Incident

This event marks the beginning of the psychological battle between Light and L. To narrow down Kira’s location, L uses a death-row inmate named Lind L. Tailor as a decoy on a live television broadcast. When Light kills the decoy, L reveals that the broadcast was only shown in the Kanto region of Japan. This proves Kira’s location and establishes that Kira needs a name and a face to kill his targets.

The Confinement and Memory Loss

To remove the growing suspicion that he is Kira, Light comes up with a complex plan: he voluntarily enters 24/7 surveillance and renounces ownership of the Death Note. This causes him to lose all his memories of being Kira. While "innocent," he works sincerely with L to catch a new Kira (from the Yotsuba Corporation). This is the only time we see Light and L working together as true allies..

The Death of L Lawliet

In a brilliant but ruthless move, Light manipulates the Shinigami Rem into a corner. To save Misa Amane from being executed by L's investigation, Rem is forced to write L’s real name in her notebook. Because a Shinigami dies if they kill to protect a human they love, Rem turns to sand, and L and Watari both die. Following this, Light takes over L's identity, pretending to lead the investigation while secretly ruling as Kira.

The Yellow Box Warehouse Confrontation

The final showdown takes place between Light and L's successor, Near. Thanks to an error made by Light’s follower, Teru Mikami, and the investigative work of Mello, Near’s agent Stephen Gevanni is able to swap the real Death Note for a perfect fake. When Mikami writes everyone’s names in the fake notebook, no one dies, exposing Light as Kira. The series ends with Ryuk writing Light’s name in his own notebook, as he promised he would at the very beginning.
